# Base Defense and Noise

## Purpose

Defense should combine infrastructure and personal response. The player must feel that the base can resist pressure, but cannot solve the war alone.

## Core statement

Defense = passive structure + active systems + player intervention.

## Scope

This spec owns:
- base defense philosophy
- passive and active defense categories
- noise/vibration as a threat amplifier

## Defense model

### Passive defense

Includes:
- walls
- stronger walls and doors
- airlock delay
- trenches or slowdown terrain

### Active defense

Includes:
- turrets with energy/ammo costs
- decoys and lures
- high-energy perimeter defense
- branch-specific late tools

### Personal intervention

The player is expected to:
- fight from emplacements
- operate heavy weapons
- leave safety when necessary
- repair under pressure

## Noise as systemic pressure

Machinery produces threat, not just output.

Key design effect:
- more generators, drills and logistics increase attack attraction
- the player balances efficiency against detectability
- silence and noise suppression are meaningful investments

## Acceptance criteria

- a well-designed base reduces danger without trivializing combat
- automation meaningfully interacts with threat pressure
- the player still matters during serious attacks

## Failure signs

- defense becomes fully automatic and passive
- or the opposite: structures barely matter
- noise exists in fiction only and does not influence decisions
