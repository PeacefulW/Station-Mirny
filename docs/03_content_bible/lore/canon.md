# Lore Canon

This is the canonical lore and world-truth document for Station Mirny.

## Scope

This file owns:
- locked world truth
- backstory
- canonical meaning of major factions/forces
- canonical late reveal
- canonical high-level glossary

This file does not own:
- low-level system implementation
- balancing numbers
- roadmap sequencing
- unresolved hypotheses

Anything still fluid belongs in [open_questions.md](open_questions.md).

## Canonical premise

Humanity no longer lives on Earth.

Earth was lost, and humanity survived aboard the Ark, a giant orbital city-ship carrying the remnants of civilization. To find a new home, the Ark sends trained Engineers to candidate worlds.

Each Engineer is a self-sufficient expedition specialist:
- technician
- builder
- scientist
- field survivor

Each Engineer carries:
- a damaged-but-vital knowledge archive
- survival and construction equipment
- the expectation of operating alone if necessary

## Player starting truth

The player character is one such Engineer.

Their descent to the planet failed. The landing became a crash. Contact with the Ark is lost. Equipment is scattered or damaged. The knowledge archive is corrupted and partially unusable.

The player's immediate truth is:
- survival first
- reconstruction second
- understanding the planet later

## The planet

The planet is hostile but not dead.

Canonical environmental traits:
- breathable only in a weak sense; oxygen is critically insufficient for normal human safety
- spores are pervasive in the atmosphere
- cold nights create serious thermal pressure
- the landscape is windswept, harsh, and visually striking rather than generically barren
- the world contains remnants of an older civilization

The intended emotional read is:
- beautiful
- dangerous
- old
- inhabited by systems and histories the player does not yet understand

## Spores

Spores are not just environmental decoration.

Canonical truths:
- they are biologically dangerous to humans
- they contaminate living bodies and mechanical systems
- they are part of the planet's core hostility
- they are deeply tied to the ancient history of the world

The spores are not merely random alien pollen. They are tied to an ancient planetary-scale biological system.

## Ancient civilization

The world once held an advanced civilization referred to in current project language as the Precursors.

Canonical truth:
- the traces found in ruins, systems, and archives belong to a real earlier civilization with direct relevance to the player's story

Detailed naming conventions, visual motifs, ruin classes, and archive taxonomy can expand later, but the existence and importance of this civilization are locked canon.

## The late reveal

This is locked canon.

The planet is not simply alien in the ordinary sense.

It is tied to humanity's origin. The ancient civilization connected to this world is not unrelated to humans; it is bound to humanity's deeper past. The spores are part of the system that once transformed, destroyed, or attempted to reclaim that civilization.

The spores do not merely "want to kill" the Engineer.
Their deeper function is closer to rollback, reversion, or forced biological return.

The meaning of the reveal is:
- the player did not arrive on a random world
- the world is a distorted ancestral home
- survival and progression are entangled with humanity's own forgotten origin

## Consequence of the reveal

The reveal changes the meaning of the two long-form paths:

### Terraformer

Canonical interpretation:
- the player rejects the world's claim over humanity
- survival means imposing human order over inherited planetary pressure
- the path is not just technical, but philosophical

### Adaptation

Canonical interpretation:
- the player stops treating the planet only as an enemy to be overwritten
- survival becomes transformation and biological acceptance
- the path is not just mutation, but a return toward buried origin

## Ruins and discovery cadence

The player should not know the full truth immediately.

Canonical reveal cadence:
1. Early hints:
   - strange ruins
   - suspiciously familiar anatomical or symbolic motifs
2. Midgame confirmation pressure:
   - analysis, scans, or archive inconsistencies suggesting human overlap
3. Late explicit confirmation:
   - recovered archives or equivalent hard proof confirm the deeper origin truth

The exact delivery systems may evolve, but the three-stage reveal structure is canon.

## Tone of the world

The world tone should remain:
- lonely
- severe
- melancholic
- awe-filled
- quietly tragic rather than campy

The setting should support the feeling that the player is uncovering something ancient, intimate, and dangerous, not merely cataloguing generic sci-fi ruins.

## Canonical glossary

### Ark
The orbital city-ship that carries what remains of humanity and sends Engineers to candidate worlds.

### Engineer
A trained human expedition specialist deployed from the Ark to survive, build, investigate, and potentially establish a future foothold.

### Precursors
The earlier civilization tied to the planet's deep past and to humanity's own hidden origin.

### Spores
The planet-wide hostile biological presence tied to contamination, mutation pressure, and the deeper ancestral reveal.

### Terraformer
The technological late-game path based on domination, infrastructure, and imposed order.

### Adaptation
The biological late-game path based on transformation, symbiosis, and return toward the world's deeper logic.
